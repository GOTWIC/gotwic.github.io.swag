About

I'm a second-year undergraduate student at New York University pursuing a joint major in Computer Science and Data Science. My research involves the intersection of machine learning and security. At NYU, I work primarily with Siddharth Garg at the Energy-Aware, Secure and Reliable Computing Research Group (ENSURE). Currently, I am working on analyzing and mitigating fairness and bias issues in Large Language Models (LLMs). My prior work has been on identifying security vulnerabilities in language models pertaining to the transfer learning pipeline, specifically backdoor attacks.

In the past two years, I have also worked with Shantanu Sharma at NJIT, Sharad Mehrotra at UCI, and Meredith Dank at the NYU Marron Institute of Urban Sciences, where my work has spanned homomorphic encryption in databases, spatial and temporal data visualization, and data analysis and visualization for disrupting the human trafficking industry. I have also interned at INVIDI technologies as a data scientist in 2022, where I worked on RNN time series forecasting and data visualization on advertisement impression data.
