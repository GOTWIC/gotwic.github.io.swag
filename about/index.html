About Research Blog

I'm a founding engineer at a robotics startup based in San Francisco, California, currently operating in stealth. I graduated from New York University Magna Cum Laude, majoring in Computer Science and Data Science. My research spans multiple topics such as natural language processing, robotics, and computer vision. Currently, I'm working on developing VLA models for domestic robots.

My primary lab at NYU was the Energy-Aware, Secure and Reliable Computing Research Group (EnSuRe) headed by Siddharth Garg, where I worked on identifying backdoor attacks on language models. My work on this topic resulted in a workshop paper at ICDE 2023.

I have also been worked with Carlos Fernandez-Granda at the Center for Data Science (CDS), where I developed large-scale vision transformers to model neuropsychiatric disorders caused by neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's.

Outside of NYU, I have worked with Aparna Varde at Montclair State University on integrating commonsense reasoning in domestic robots, in order to make task planning more efficient in human-robot collaborative tasks. Our work was accepted at MIT URTC 2024.

In the past two years, I have also worked with Shantanu Sharma at NJIT, Sharad Mehrotra at UCI, and Meredith Dank at the NYU Marron Institute of Urban Sciences, where my work has spanned homomorphic encryption in databases, spatial and temporal data visualization, and data analysis and visualization for disrupting the human trafficking industry.

Before entering college and starting my research career, I interned full-time at INVIDI Technologies as a data scientist in Summer 2022, where I worked on RNN time series forecasting and data visualization on advertisement impression data.
